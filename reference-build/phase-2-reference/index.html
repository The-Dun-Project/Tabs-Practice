Days 6–7 · Directional Slide

Panel One

Directional awareness

Click the tabs in order, then in reverse. Watch how the content slides in the direction of your navigation. The interface tells you where you are going, not just what changed.

Panel Two

Left or right?

JavaScript compares the index of the tab you clicked to the index of the currently active tab. If the new index is higher, you moved right. Lower means left. The content enters from the same direction you navigated.

Panel Three

Direction is information

You are now managing not just WHAT is active but the relationship between the previous state and the next one. This is a meaningful step forward from a simple fade.

Day 8 · Animated Height

Short content

Small panel

This panel has very little content. Switch to Tall and watch the container grow smoothly without jumping.

Medium content

A bit more here

This panel has a moderate amount of content. It is taller than Short but shorter than Tall. Watch the container resize as you switch — it grows and shrinks without any jumping.

Tall content

Lots of content in this one

This panel is significantly taller than the others. Without height animation, the container would snap to its new size instantly. With animation, it grows naturally.

JavaScript reads this panel's scrollHeight — its full content height even while hidden — and sets that as a pixel value on the container. CSS transition does the rest.

This is one of the trickier animation patterns because height: auto cannot be transitioned. You always need an explicit pixel value to animate from and to.

Days 9–10 · Keyboard Navigation

Keyboard · Alpha

Click me, then press arrow keys

Click any tab in this section. Then press your left and right arrow keys. You can navigate all three tabs without touching the mouse. This is how accessible interfaces work.

Keyboard · Beta

You pressed an arrow key

JavaScript listened for keydown on the tab list. It checked event.key — if ArrowRight or ArrowLeft, it calculated the next index and called .focus() and .click() on that button.

Keyboard · Gamma

It wraps around

Right on the last tab goes back to the first. Left on the first goes to the last. Users should never get stuck at the edge of a component.
